The PM2.5 results indicate a direct link between temporal autocorrelation structure and the observed Skill(h) profile. Specifically, when the ACF decays slowly, persistence remains a strong baseline over short lead times, which raises the threshold that alternative models must exceed to obtain positive relative skill. This mechanism explains why PM2.5 persistence forecasts remain competitive and why model skill tends to become positive only at longer horizons, where short-term autocorrelation-driven persistence advantage weakens.
