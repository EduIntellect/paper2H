This research prioritizes the characterization of operational predictability limits rather than the sole minimization of forecast error. Accordingly, the central objective is to estimate how far ahead a system can be predicted with meaningful advantage over a naive reference, across heterogeneous real-world domains. The proposed metric, H*, formalizes this perspective as the maximum forecast horizon at which model skill remains positive relative to a baseline, providing an interpretable measure of practical predictability beyond conventional point-accuracy optimization.
